Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

The short version: Medical Casebook does not collect any data. We have no servers, no analytics, no accounts, and no advertising. Everything you create in the app stays on your device and, if you use iCloud, in your own private iCloud account — which we cannot access.

Data we collect

None. Medical Casebook does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information or usage data. Per Apple's App Privacy definitions, the app's privacy label is "Data Not Collected": no data linked to you, no data not linked to you, and no data used to track you.

Where your content lives

Your questions, tags, sources, synthesis notes, and case images are stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. If you are signed in to iCloud, the app syncs this content across your devices using Apple's CloudKit private database. That data belongs to your Apple Account; it is encrypted in transit, governed by Apple's privacy policy, and is technically inaccessible to the developer of this app.

Speech recognition

Voice capture uses Apple's on-device speech recognition. Audio is transcribed locally on your device and is not sent to us or to any third-party service by the app.

Network requests

The app works fully offline, with one optional exception: when you add a source by DOI or URL, the app contacts public bibliographic services (such as the CrossRef API) or the website you entered to fetch citation metadata (title, authors, journal). These requests contain only the identifier you typed — never your notes, images, identity, or any device identifiers. If you never add a source, the app makes no network requests at all.

Analytics, tracking, and advertising

  • No third-party analytics or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • No advertising, ad networks, or tracking of any kind.
  • No cookies, fingerprinting, or device identifiers.

Accounts

The app requires no account, registration, or login. We never see your email address, name, or any other identifier.

Children's privacy

Medical Casebook is intended for medical professionals and trainees. Because the app collects no data from anyone, it likewise collects no data from children.

Data deletion

Because we hold no data about you, there is nothing for us to delete. You can remove all app content at any time by deleting content within the app, deleting the app itself, and — if you use iCloud sync — removing the app's data from iCloud in Settings > Apple Account > iCloud > Manage Account Storage.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app ever changes how data is handled, this page and the App Store privacy label will be updated before that version ships, with the date above revised.
